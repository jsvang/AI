1. AI Fundamentals

What is Artificial Intelligence (AI)?

At its core, AI refers to computational systems performing tasks typically associated with human intelligence. This is a broad field of computer science building machines that can reason, learn, and act. It encompasses various technologies for advanced functions like language interpretation, data analysis, and providing recommendations.

Learning & Adaptation

Unlike traditional programs, AI systems learn and adapt from data, continuously analyzing datasets to identify patterns and improve performance over time.

Types of Intelligence

All current AI is "Narrow" (ANI), designed for specific tasks. "General" AI (AGI), which would be human-like in intelligence, is still theoretical.

3. Deep Dive into LLMs

Demystifying Large Language Models (LLMs)

Large Language Models (LLMs) are the advanced AI systems powering most modern conversational AI. They are trained on immense amounts of text data, allowing them to understand and generate human-like language with incredible sophistication. Their core technology is based on deep learning and a breakthrough "transformer architecture."

Important Considerations: LLM Limitations

While powerful, LLMs have critical limitations you must understand to use them responsibly. Always apply your own judgment and critical thinking.

🧠 Lack of Common Sense

LLMs find statistical patterns, but they don't truly understand context or possess common-sense reasoning. Their outputs can be illogical.

👻 Hallucinations

LLMs can generate plausible-sounding but incorrect or completely fabricated information. Always verify critical facts.

⚖️ Bias

Trained on real-world data, LLMs can reflect and amplify societal biases. Review outputs for fairness.

6. Mastering the Craft: Prompt Engineering

What is Prompt Engineering?

Prompt engineering is the art and science of crafting effective inputs ("prompts") to guide AI models toward your desired output. It’s the key to unlocking consistent, high-quality, and accurate results. Think of it as learning how to have a clear and precise conversation with the AI.

Best Practices:

  • Be Specific & Provide Context: Long, detailed prompts are often better.
  • Give the Model Room to Think: Ask it to reason "step-by-step."
  • Quote Sources: Ask the model to cite sources to reduce hallucinations.
  • Clarify the Format: Tell it if you want a table, bullet points, etc.

Key Prompting Techniques

Explore these techniques to enhance your results. Click each tab to learn more.

Zero-Shot Prompting

Asking the model to perform a task without giving it any examples. It relies solely on its pre-trained knowledge.

Example: "Explain the concept of climate change in simple terms."

Few-Shot Prompting

Including a few examples in your prompt to guide the model on the desired format, tone, or style of the output.

Example: "Translate 'hello' to French as 'bonjour'. Now translate 'goodbye'."

Chain of Thought (CoT) Prompting

Encouraging the model to reason through a problem step-by-step before giving a final answer. This improves accuracy on complex tasks.

Example: "First, identify the capital of France. Second, list its main tourist attractions. Combine this into a paragraph."

Meta Prompting

Asking the model to generate or refine its own prompt for a task. This can help you create a more effective prompt for future use.

Example: "Create a detailed prompt that will cause you to generate a comprehensive report on renewable energy sources."
